We know the angle from the surface is $18^{\circ}$ $\implies$ we know $\theta_{1} = 90-18 = 72^{\circ}$. Then we have:

$$
\begin{align}
\frac{\sin(\theta_{1})}{\sin(\theta_{2})} & = \frac{n_{2}}{n_{1}}\\[1em]
\sin(\theta_{2}) & = \frac{n_{1}\cdot\sin(\theta_{1})}{n_{2}} \\[1em]
\sin(\theta_{2}) & = \frac{1\cdot\sin(72)}{1.54} \\[1em]
\sin(\theta_{2}) & = 0.617569... \\[1em]
\theta_{2} & = \sin^{-1}(0.617569...) = 38.1^{\circ}
\end{align}
$$
